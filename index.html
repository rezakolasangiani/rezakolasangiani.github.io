About Me

Background & Expertise

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Chao Lab at Harvard Medical School, where I continue my work at the interface of computational biology, mechanobiology, and therapeutic discovery.

I completed my PhD and MS in Bioengineering at the University of Utah (GPA 3.98/4.0), where I spent four years in the Computational Biology Lab investigating integrin mechanosensing through microsecond-scale all-atom molecular dynamics simulations. My doctoral work produced 5+ first-author publications and podium presentations at leading international conferences including the Biophysical Society Annual Meeting.

My research bridges structural biology, biophysics, and machine learning — from elucidating the conformational states of αIIbβ3 and αVβ3 integrins to designing structure-based approaches for targeting integrins in cancer therapy.

Research

My Research Focus

I use molecular dynamics simulations and computational modeling to understand how proteins sense and transduce mechanical signals — with applications in cancer therapy, thrombosis, and organoid development.

Integrin Mechanobiology

Investigating how integrins αIIbβ3 and αVβ3 change conformation in response to mechanical force and biochemical ligands. Using over 1 µs of all-atom MD simulations to elucidate novel activation states and force-transduction pathways at the cell–matrix interface.

αIIbβ3 · αVβ3 Integrins Force Transduction GROMACS

Molecular Dynamics Simulations

Developing and running equilibrium and enhanced sampling MD simulations of membrane proteins. Building Python-based automated pipelines for trajectory analysis, free-energy calculations, and protein–ligand interaction profiling.

Enhanced Sampling Free-Energy Calc. Python Pipelines

Computational Drug Design

Applying structure-based approaches and machine learning to discover novel integrin agonists and therapeutic antibodies. Using AlphaFold and HADDOCK to model antibody–receptor interactions and identify drug candidates for cancer and inflammation.

AlphaFold · HADDOCK Cancer Therapy ML for Drug Discovery
